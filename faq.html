Faqs

HOW CAN I PAY FOR MY APPOINTMENT?
We accept cash, credit cards, and Debit cards.
WILL YOU FIX OR COVER AN OLD TATTOO?
At NH Custom Tattoo, we aim to provide all of our guests with a top notch experience. Schedule a consultation immediately so that we may discuss the options to turn your tattoo nightmare into something special!
CAN I CONSUME ALCOHOL BEFOREHAND?
Refrain from drinking alchohol prior to your appointment as it thins your blood. It is against state law to tattoo someone under the influence.
DO YOU HAVE ANY TATTOO TREATMENT AND AFTERCARE TIPS?
Keeping the tattoo clean and dry is important. 2 hours after getting inked, clean it with hot water and anti-bacterial soap. Firmly pat the skin dry and apply pressure to the wound. Before you go to bed, wear clothing that is clean to prevent it from acquiring any cross contaminants on the wound. Use an unscented lotion to moisturize the skin. You might be tempted to show it off and go about your day and not give your tattoo the attention that it needs anymore...DON’T. Being impatient won’t do your tattoo any good. If you just can't help it, then take a shot of your ink, post it on social media, and tag us on your post! Right after getting inked, your skin would react to the inflicted wounds. And if you are going outside with a tank top, make sure to apply Tattoo Goo moisturizer or sunscreen only after it has healed to your tattoo to avoid dehydrating it.
DO YOU REQUIRE A DEPOSIT?
To make an appointment, our artists take a $50-$100 non-refundable deposit. That will go towards the balance of the tattoo. We require 72 hours notice to cancel and reschedule you appointment, if adequate notice is not given, your deposit will be forfeit.
DO YOU ALLOW VISITORS?
Yes, feel free to bring your friends along for the ride. NH Custom is equipped with comfortable reclining chairs and a high end entertainment system to pass the time in style.
DO YOU TAKE WALK-INS?
Due to Covid-19 please call the shop for the most updated information regarding walkins. All walk-ins are handled on a first-come first-serve basis. It's always best to call to the shop and ask what our availability looks like that day.
